# A Survey of Deep Learning: From Activations to Transformers

## Paper Details

- **Title**: A Survey of Deep Learning: From Activations to Transformers
- **Authors**: Johannes Schneider, Michalis Vlachos
- **Venue**: International Conference on Agents and Artificial Intelligence (ICAART), 2024
- **arXiv**: 2302.00722
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.00722

## Summary

A comprehensive survey of deep learning covering key architectural innovations over the last decade. The paper identifies patterns that summarize successful strategies and discusses emerging areas including transformers and self-supervised learning.

## Key Topics Covered

### Architectural Components
- **Activation functions**: ReLU, GELU, SwiGLU
- **Normalization**: BatchNorm, LayerNorm, RMSNorm
- **Skip connections**: Residual connections, skip connections

### Attention Mechanisms
- **Self-attention**: Core mechanism in transformers
- **Multi-head attention**: Parallel attention heads
- **Sparse attention**: Reducing quadratic complexity

### Transformers
- **Architecture**: Encoder-decoder, GPT-style, BERT-style
- **Variants**: Vision Transformers (ViT), efficient transformers
- **Large models**: GPT-4, PaLM 2 discussion

### Training Techniques
- **Self-supervised learning**: Contrastive, masked prediction
- **Pretraining + finetuning**: Transfer learning paradigm
- **Optimization**: Learning rate schedules, Adam variants

## Identified Patterns

The survey identifies multiple recurring patterns in successful innovations:
1. Skip connections enable deeper networks
2. Normalization stabilizes training
3. Attention provides flexible modeling
4. Scaling laws for large models
